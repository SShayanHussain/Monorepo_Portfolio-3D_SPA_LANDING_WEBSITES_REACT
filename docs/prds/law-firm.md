# PRD — Law firm

## Client profile (fictional)
"Hartwell & Cole" — a mid-size firm, practice areas in business/civil litigation, the kind of firm that needs to project authority and discretion simultaneously. Legal clients are buying trust more than any other niche in this portfolio.

## Goal of this site
Generate qualified consultation requests from people who are often in a stressful situation (legal need) and need to feel confident this firm can handle it. Primary conversion: "Request a consultation". Secondary: browse practice areas.

## Sections (in order)
1. **Hero** — restrained 3D scene (see below), firm name, authoritative headline, CTA "Request a consultation" + secondary "View practice areas".
2. **Practice areas** — grid of 4-8 practice areas (litigation, corporate, real estate law, family law, etc., adjust to firm focus), name + short description, links to deeper info.
3. **Why this firm** — track record stats (cases won, years practicing, client retention) as a clean stat row, animated count-up on scroll.
4. **Attorney profiles** — photo, name, title, bar admissions, education, short bio — credentials matter enormously in this niche, don't abbreviate them.
5. **Case results / testimonials** — anonymized case outcomes or client testimonials (note: many jurisdictions have ethics rules about advertising specific case results — flag this as a real-client consideration, use generic placeholder framing for the portfolio piece).
6. **Consultation request form** — name, contact, practice area needed, brief case description, clear note about attorney-client privilege/confidentiality on the form itself (this reassurance measurably improves form completion in legal).
7. **Footer** — office address, map, phone, disclaimer text (legal sites typically need an "attorney advertising" disclaimer — include a placeholder).

## 3D concept
This is the most restrained 3D application in the portfolio, deliberately. Authority is communicated through precision and stillness, not motion. Concept: a minimal, geometric architectural object — think a simplified abstracted column, scale/balance motif, or a precise geometric solid (could be literal: a stylized scale-of-justice abstraction rendered as clean intersecting planes, not a literal ornate scale icon) rendered with sharp, confident lighting, near-static with only the most subtle ambient rotation (extremely slow, barely perceptible, more presence than animation). This should never feel "flashy" — if it reads as showing off, it's wrong for this niche. The restraint itself is the design choice that differentiates this PRD from the others.

Strongly consider whether 3D earns its place here at all relative to other niches — flag in memory.md if, after building a few other sites, the law firm site reads better with a static high-quality image/subtle CSS treatment instead of a mounted Canvas. This PRD permits that judgment call; the brief's request for "3D across the portfolio" is satisfied by other sites carrying that weight more clearly, and forcing 3D into every single niche risks the law firm site feeling like 3D-for-3D's-sake.

Mobile fallback: static image of the same geometric composition, true for all viewport sizes if the judgment above lands on "minimal or no 3D" for this niche.

## Palette & type direction
Deep, conservative — navy, charcoal, a single muted gold or burgundy accent used sparingly (not as a dominant color, as a precision accent on CTAs/dividers only). Display font: a serif with gravity (think the kind of serif used on legal letterhead) — this is the one niche in the portfolio where a traditional serif outperforms a modern sans for credibility signaling. Body: clean sans for readability, serif reserved for headlines/credentials.

## Tone of voice for copy
Measured, precise, no hyperbole. Avoid "the best", "unmatched", "aggressive representation" clichés. Specificity over superlative — name the actual practice areas and actual experience rather than vague claims of excellence.

## Conversion priorities
1. "Request a consultation" CTA present but not aggressive in styling — match the restrained brand, no urgency-manufacturing language ("act now") which reads as untrustworthy in this niche specifically
2. Attorney credentials must be fully visible, not truncated behind a "read more" — bar admissions and education are trust signals people specifically look for
3. Confidentiality/privilege reassurance near the contact form, explicitly stated

## Performance notes specific to this site
Likely the lightest 3D footprint in the portfolio by design (see above). If the minimal-3D judgment call is made, this site should comfortably be the fastest-loading of the eight — worth using as the internal benchmark for "what does the budget look like when 3D is barely used" versus the gym/fitness site as "what does it look like fully leaned into."
